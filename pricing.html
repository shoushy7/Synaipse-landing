Why We Split It This Way

Independent physicians shouldn't pay RCM rates on tools that have nothing to do with collections. We separated them so you never overpay.

SaaS = Predictable Fixed Cost

Credentialing, intake, EHR integration, and analytics are operational tools — they don't fluctuate with your collections. A flat per-provider fee means you know exactly what you're paying every month. No surprises.

Revenue Share = Aligned Incentives

Billing, denials, underpayment recovery, and collections directly impact your revenue. We only earn when you earn more. Our rates are a fraction of the 5–10% legacy RCM companies charge — and our AI delivers faster, more accurate results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are SaaS and Revenue Share separate?

Because they solve different problems. Practice automation tools (credentialing, intake, EHR integration) are operational — you need them running regardless of claim volume. A fixed monthly fee makes sense. Revenue cycle tasks (billing, denials, collections) directly impact your revenue, so a performance-based model aligns our incentives with yours. You only pay a percentage on the revenue we help recover — and that percentage is well below what legacy RCM companies charge.

How does the revenue share percentage compare to legacy RCM?

Traditional RCM companies typically charge 5–10% of your total collections, whether they improve anything or not. Synaipse charges a significantly lower percentage, and only on incremental revenue we help you recover above your current baseline. That means you never pay for performance you were already achieving — and you keep a far greater share of every dollar collected.

Do I need both models, or can I pick one?

You can use either model independently. Many independent physicians start with just the SaaS subscription for credentialing and practice tools, then add Revenue Share when they're ready to optimize their billing cycle. Others start with Revenue Share first to see immediate ROI with zero upfront risk, then add SaaS for the operational tools. We'll help you find the right fit during a free consultation.

I'm a solo practitioner — is this built for me?

Absolutely. Synaipse was designed by physicians who understand the unique challenges of independent practice. Solo practitioners and small groups often lose the most to administrative burden because they don't have dedicated billing teams. Our SaaS tools eliminate hours of manual credentialing and intake work, and our Revenue Share model recovers revenue that would otherwise slip through the cracks — denied claims, underpayments, and coding gaps you may not have the bandwidth to chase.

What's the typical ROI timeline?

SaaS tools deliver immediate time savings — credentialing tasks that took hours happen in minutes. On the Revenue Share side, most practices see measurable revenue recovery within the first 30–60 days. Denial recovery shows material impact within 60–90 days as appeals are processed and collected. You'll receive transparent monthly reports showing exactly what's been recovered.

What EHR systems do you integrate with?

Synaipse integrates bidirectionally with all major EHR platforms including Epic, Cerner (Oracle Health), athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, Allscripts, NextGen, and Greenway. Our FHIR-based architecture means we can connect to any certified EHR system. For independent practices, our athenahealth and eClinicalWorks integrations are particularly robust.

Is there a long-term contract?

The SaaS subscription is available monthly or annually (with a discount for annual commitment). The Revenue Share model uses a 12-month initial term so the AI has sufficient time to learn your practice's payer mix and denial patterns and deliver maximum value. Both models include a 90-day satisfaction guarantee — if you're not seeing results, you walk away.

How does pricing scale as my practice grows?

Growth benefits both models. SaaS per-provider pricing decreases as you add providers. The Revenue Share percentage stays fixed, but larger practices see greater absolute recovery — meaning both sides benefit from scale. Many practices start as solo or small-group and grow with Synaipse handling the administrative complexity.
