Frequently Asked Questions

How does the revenue share model work exactly?

We establish a baseline of your current collections and denial rates during onboarding. Our AI agents then work to recover revenue from denied claims, underpayments, and collection gaps. We take a pre-agreed percentage only on the incremental revenue recovered above your baseline — meaning you never pay for performance you were already achieving.

Can I switch between pricing models?

Yes. Many customers start with the revenue share model to prove ROI with zero risk, then transition to a flat SaaS subscription once they've seen the results. We make the transition seamless and there's no interruption to your workflows.

What's the typical ROI timeline?

Most practices see measurable results within the first 30 days. Prior authorization time savings are immediate. Denial recovery typically shows material impact within 60–90 days as appeals are processed and collected. On the revenue share model, you'll start seeing recovered revenue in your first monthly report.

Is there a minimum practice size?

The SaaS subscription is available for practices of any size. The revenue share model works best for practices with at least 5 providers, where the volume of claims creates enough recovery opportunity to make the model beneficial for both parties. We'll help you determine which model fits best during our initial consultation.

What EHR systems do you integrate with?

Synaipse integrates bidirectionally with all major EHR platforms including Epic, Cerner (Oracle Health), athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, Allscripts, NextGen, and Greenway. Our FHIR-based architecture means we can connect to any certified EHR system.

Is there a long-term contract?

The SaaS subscription is offered on annual terms with the option to pay monthly. The revenue share model operates on a 12-month initial term to allow sufficient time for the AI to learn your practice's patterns and deliver maximum value. Both models include a satisfaction guarantee during the first 90 days.

How is pricing affected as we add more providers?

Volume benefits both models. On the SaaS plan, per-provider pricing decreases as your organization grows. On the revenue share plan, the percentage remains fixed, but larger practices typically see greater absolute recovery — meaning both parties benefit from scale.
