Why Three Packages

Independent physicians shouldn't pay RCM rates on tools that have nothing to do with collections. We separated them so you never overpay.

Automation = Predictable Fixed Cost

Intake, clinical visit intelligence, and surgery scheduling are operational tools that don't fluctuate with your collections. A flat per-provider fee means you know exactly what you're paying every month.

Credentialing = Only If You Need It

Not every practice needs credentialing help. By offering it as a standalone add-on, practices that already have enrollment sorted aren't forced to pay for it — and those that do get a purpose-built solution.

Revenue Share = Aligned Incentives

Prior authorization, billing, denials, and collections directly impact your revenue. We only earn when you earn more. Our rates are a fraction of the 5–10% legacy RCM companies charge.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why three separate packages instead of one bundled price?

Because they solve different problems. Practice Automation (intake, clinical visit, surgery scheduling) is operational — you need it running regardless of claim volume, so a flat fee makes sense. Credentialing is a distinct, specialized process that not every practice needs help with — so we offer it as a standalone add-on. Revenue cycle management (prior auth, billing, denials) directly impacts your revenue, so a performance-based model aligns our incentives: you only pay a percentage on the revenue we help recover.

How does the revenue share percentage compare to legacy RCM?

Traditional RCM companies typically charge 5–10% of your total collections, whether they improve anything or not. Synaipse charges a significantly lower percentage, and only on incremental revenue we help you recover above your current baseline. That means you never pay for performance you were already achieving — and you keep a far greater share of every dollar collected.

Do I need all three packages, or can I pick and choose?

Each package works independently. Many independent physicians start with just Practice Automation for intake and clinical workflow, then add Revenue Share when they're ready to optimize billing. If you already have credentialing covered, skip it entirely. Others start with Revenue Share to see immediate ROI with zero upfront risk. We'll help you find the right combination during a free consultation.

I'm a solo practitioner — is this built for me?

Absolutely. Synaipse was designed by physicians who understand the unique challenges of independent practice. Solo practitioners and small groups lose the most to administrative burden because they don't have dedicated billing teams. Practice Automation eliminates hours of manual intake and scheduling work, and Revenue Share recovers revenue that would otherwise slip through the cracks — denied claims, underpayments, and coding gaps you may not have bandwidth to chase.

What's the typical ROI timeline?

Practice Automation delivers immediate time savings — intake tasks that took hours happen in minutes. On the Revenue Share side, most practices see measurable revenue recovery within the first 30–60 days. Denial recovery shows material impact within 60–90 days as appeals are processed and collected. You'll receive transparent monthly reports showing exactly what's been recovered.

What EHR systems do you integrate with?

Synaipse integrates bidirectionally with all major EHR platforms including Epic, Cerner (Oracle Health), athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, Allscripts, NextGen, and Greenway. Our FHIR-based architecture means we can connect to any certified EHR system. For independent practices, our athenahealth and eClinicalWorks integrations are particularly robust.

Is there a long-term contract?

Practice Automation and Credentialing are available monthly or annually (with a discount for annual commitment). The Revenue Share model uses a 12-month initial term so the AI has sufficient time to learn your practice's payer mix and denial patterns and deliver maximum value. All packages include a 90-day satisfaction guarantee — if you're not seeing results, you walk away.

How does pricing scale as my practice grows?

Growth benefits all packages. Practice Automation and Credentialing per-provider pricing decreases as you add providers. The Revenue Share percentage stays fixed, but larger practices see greater absolute recovery — meaning both sides benefit from scale. Many practices start as solo or small-group and grow with Synaipse handling the administrative complexity.
